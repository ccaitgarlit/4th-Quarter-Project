ASTRONOMY

Astronomy Importance History

WHAT IS IT?

Astronomy is the scientific study of celestial objects and the universe. It encompasses the observation, measurement, and analysis of stars, planets, galaxies, and other phenomena beyond Earth. Astronomy has a wide scope, including astrophysics, planetary science, and cosmology.

WHY IS IT IMPORTANT?

The importance of studying astronomy lies in its ability to expand our understanding of the cosmos. It reveals the fundamental principles that govern the universe and deepens our knowledge of our place within it. Astronomy also drives technological advancements, leading to innovations and spin-off technologies that benefit society. It contributes to addressing global challenges such as climate, space weather, and sustainable solutions.

HOW DID IT START?

Ancient Astronomy

Our perceptions of the Solar System and space have evolved over time. For thousands of years, people have looked to the heavens. They studied the movement of the stars as well as the changes in the Sun and Moon. Star maps were created using what was known about the positions of stars, eclipses, and Moon phases. This data was used to create calendars and measure time. It was extremely beneficial for determining when to plant crops or for navigating.
